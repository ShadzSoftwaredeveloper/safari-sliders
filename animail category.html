Rhino

The rhinoceros is the largest land animal after the elephant. Modern rhinos reach a length of 2–5 meters and weigh from 1 to 3.6 tons.

Best places to see:
  • Hluhluwe–Imfolozi Park
  • Kruger National Park

Cheetah

The cheetah is the fastest animal in the world. When chasing prey, it can reach speeds of up to 112 kilometers per hour.

Best places to see:
  • Masai Mara National Park
  • Amboseli National Park

Elephant

Elephants are giants among animals. Their weight ranges from 3 to 7 tons, with African elephants often reaching 10–12 tons.

Best places to see:
  • Chobe National Park
  • Kruger National Park

Zebra

Zebras reach more than 2 meters in length and weigh 300–350 kg. They have poor eyesight but a well‑developed sense of smell.

Best places to see:
  • Hluhluwe–Imfolozi Park
  • Etosha National Park
